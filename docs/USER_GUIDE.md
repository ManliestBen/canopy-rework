# 🌳 Canopy — Family Guide

Welcome to Canopy, our family's wall calendar! 👋 This is the big touchscreen
on the wall, and this guide walks you through *everything* it can do — one
step at a time.

Two things to know before we start:

- **Everything works by touch.** Just tap. No double-taps, no press-and-hold,
  no mouse. If you can tap it, it does something. 😊
- **You can't really break it.** Tap around and explore. If you change
  something by accident, there's almost always an *undo*, a *Cancel*, or a
  way to change it right back.

This guide is long on purpose — but you don't have to read it all. Jump to the
section you need using the list below.

> 💡 **The same guide lives inside the panel.** Tap the **❓** button in the
> top bar any time to see a shorter version right on screen.

## 🧭 Getting around

**What it is:** How you move between Canopy's screens and read the top bar.

### What you'll see

- **A bar of icons down the left edge.** Each icon is one screen. Tap an icon
  to go there; the icon you're on is highlighted.
- **A top bar across the very top.** From left to right it shows: our **family
  name**, the **time** (like `4:30 PM`), the **date** (like *Wednesday, July
  15*), then a few little buttons and, on the far right, a **colored circle for
  each person** in the family.

### The left icon bar

| Icon | Screen | What it's for |
|---|---|---|
| 📅 | **Calendar** | Everyone's events — the main screen |
| 🧹 | **Chores** | The daily chore chart |
| ⭐ | **Rewards** | Stars you've earned and what you can spend |
| ✅ | **To-Dos** | The family to-do list |
| 🍽️ | **Meals** | What's for dinner this week |
| 🛒 | **Lists** | Shopping and other lists |
| 🖼️ | **Photos** | Family photos and the slideshow |
| 🌙 | **Sleep** | What the panel does at night |
| ⚙️ | **Settings** | Grown-up stuff (may ask for the PIN) |

### The top-bar buttons

- **❓ (question mark)** — opens the in-app family guide. Great for a quick
  refresher.
- **⏱ (stopwatch)** — opens the [countdown Timer](#-timer).
- **📣 (megaphone)** — posts a [sticky note](#-notes-announcements) on the main
  screen.
- **The temperature** (like `72°`) — tap it for the full
  [weather forecast](#-weather).
- **The colored circles** — these are us! Each person has **one color** that
  follows them everywhere: their events, chores, list items, and notes all use
  that same color, so you can tell at a glance whose is whose.

### The "⚠ Offline" badge

If the internet hiccups, a small **⚠ Offline** badge appears in the top bar.
Don't worry — the panel keeps showing the last thing it knew, and it **catches
up on its own** the moment the connection comes back. There's nothing you need
to do.

### How to move to a different screen

1. Look at the icon bar on the left edge.
2. Tap the icon for the screen you want.
3. That's it — the screen changes right away.

## 📅 Calendar

**What it is:** Everyone's events in one place. This is the main screen and the
one you'll look at most.

### What you'll see

- Each event is a **colored pill** (a rounded label). The **color** tells you
  which calendar it came from, and a **little face** on the pill shows whose
  event it is.
- **Long events** (a trip, a camp) stretch across several days as a banner.
- In Day and Week views, a **red line** runs across the grid to show **"right
  now."**
- **Weather** appears on each day's header, so you can plan the picnic with
  confidence. ☀️
- If you have notes posted, a **strip of sticky notes** sits at the top (see
  [Notes](#-notes-announcements)).

### The five views

Along the top you'll find buttons to switch how the calendar looks:

- **Agenda** — a simple "what's coming up" list for about the next two weeks.
  Perfect for a quick glance.
- **Day** — one day, hour by hour.
- **Week** — the whole week at once (this is the usual starting view).
- **2 Weeks** — two weeks side by side.
- **Month** — the full month as a grid.

#### How to change the view

1. Look at the row of view buttons near the top (**Agenda · Day · Week · 2
   Weeks · Month**).
2. Tap the one you want. The highlighted button is the view you're on.

#### How to move to other dates

1. Use the **‹** and **›** arrows near the top to step backward or forward
   (by a day, week, or month depending on the view).
2. Tap **Today** any time to jump straight back to today.

*(The Agenda view always starts from today, so it doesn't have the arrows.)*

### How to see just one person's events

1. At the top of the calendar you'll see everyone's colored circles.
2. **Tap your circle.** The calendar now spotlights only your events (plus
   shared family events that belong to no one in particular).
3. Tap your circle again to go back to showing everyone.

> This only appears when there's more than one family member.

### How to add an event

There are **three easy ways** — pick whichever is handiest:

1. **The blue + button** in the bottom-right corner. Tap it and a "New event"
   box opens, starting on today.
2. **Tap an empty time slot** in Day or Week view. The box opens with that
   day and time already filled in for you.
3. **Tap a day's name** at the top of a column. The box opens on that day.

Then fill in the details:

1. **What** — the event's name. Emoji are welcome and make things easy to
   spot: 🦷 Dentist, ⚽ Soccer, 🎂 Birthday.
2. **Calendar** — if we have more than one calendar you can write to, pick
   which one this event goes on.
3. **All day** — tick this for things with no set time (a birthday, a day
   off). If it's off, you'll set a start and end **time**.
4. **Starts / Ends** — choose the dates (and times, unless it's all-day).
5. **Repeats** — see below.
6. **Where** and **Notes** — both optional, for a location and any extra
   details.
7. Tap **Save**. (The Save button turns on once the event has a name and the
   times make sense.)

### How to make an event repeat

1. In the event box, find the **Repeats** menu.
2. Choose one: **Does not repeat**, **Every day**, **Every weekday (Mon–Fri)**,
   **Weekly**, **Monthly**, or **Yearly**. Weekly is perfect for soccer
   practice; Yearly is perfect for birthdays. 🎂
3. Tap **Save**.

> **Fancy repeat rules are safe.** If an event has a repeat pattern more
> unusual than the menu covers (often ones that came from Google), Canopy
> shows it as **"kept as-is"** and leaves that rule **exactly** as it is — it
> never rewrites it. You can still edit the event's other details.

### How to change or delete an event

1. **Tap the event** on the calendar. A details box opens.
2. Tap **Edit** to change anything, then **Save**.
3. To remove it, tap **Delete**, then confirm. For a repeating event the
   button says **"Really delete entire series?"** so you know it removes all of
   them.

### If something's off

- **The blue + button isn't there / you can't add events.** Canopy needs a
  calendar it's allowed to write to (a Google calendar). School and team
  calendars we *subscribe* to are **read-only** — you can look but not change
  them (edit those on the school's own website). A grown-up can add a writable
  calendar from **Calendar → Manage**.
- **A yellow warning strip appears.** A calendar had trouble updating. Canopy
  keeps showing the **last known events** and will refresh on its own.
- **The screen says "Let's connect your calendars."** No calendars are set up
  yet — tap **Add a calendar** (a grown-up can finish this in about a minute).

## 🧹 Chores

**What it is:** The family chore chart. Everyone has their own column and taps
to check chores off.

### What you'll see

- **One column per person**, tinted in that person's color, with their name
  and picture at the top.
- A little **count** like `2/4` shows how many chores that person has finished.
- A **progress bar** fills up in their color as they check things off.
- Each chore is a **pill** showing its icon, name, how often it repeats, and
  how many **⭐ stars** it's worth.

### How to complete a chore

1. Find your column.
2. **Tap the circle** on the right side of the chore.
3. It turns into a **green ✓** and your progress bar fills a little more. 🎉
4. Your stars are added automatically (see [Rewards](#-rewards)).

### How to undo a chore

- Tapped one by mistake? **Tap the green ✓ again** and it goes back to
  un-done. No harm done.

### How to look at another day

1. Use the **‹** and **›** arrows at the top to step back or forward a day.
2. Tap **Today** to come back to today.

### How grown-ups add or edit chores

1. Tap **+ Add chore** at the top.
2. Type the **Chore** name (e.g. *Make bed*).
3. Pick an **Icon** if you like (bed, broom, dishes, trash, dog, books, and
   more — or none).
4. Choose **Who** it's for by tapping their circle.
5. Set **Repeats**: *Every day*, *Weekdays*, or *Weekly (from today)*.
6. Set **Stars**: 1, 2, 3, 5, or 10 ⭐.
7. Tap **Save**.

To change a chore later, **tap the chore's name** to open it, edit anything,
and Save — or tap **Delete** to remove it.

### If something's off

- **A column is empty ("No chores today 🎉").** That person simply has nothing
  scheduled for the day you're looking at.
- **Nobody has a column.** No family members have been added yet — a grown-up
  can add them in **Settings → Family members**.

## ⭐ Rewards

**What it is:** Where the stars from chores add up, and where you spend them.

### What you'll see

- **One card per person**, in their color, showing:
  - Their **star balance** (the big ⭐ number — what they can spend right now).
  - How many they **earned this week** and **all time**.
- Below the cards, a **"Recently spent"** list of the latest redemptions, so
  there are no arguments. 😄

### How to spend stars

1. Find your card.
2. Tap **Spend stars**. (This button is greyed out if you have 0 stars.)
3. Enter **how many** stars to spend.
4. Type **what for** — like *"Movie night pick!"* or *"Ice cream run."*
5. Tap **Spend** to confirm.

Your balance goes down by that many stars, and the redemption shows up in
"Recently spent."

### If something's off

- **"Spend stars" is greyed out.** That person has no stars yet — do a few
  chores first!
- **The balance looks low.** Remember it's *balance*, not lifetime total.
  Spending stars lowers the balance but the **all-time** number keeps climbing.

## ✅ To-Dos

**What it is:** The family to-do list, sorted so the important stuff floats to
the top.

### What you'll see

Tasks are grouped into sections, in this order:

- **Overdue** (shown in red) — past their due date.
- **Today** — due today.
- **Coming up** — due later.
- **Someday** — no due date, just "eventually."

Each to-do shows its name, and underneath, small details like its repeat
schedule, due date, or category. If a to-do belongs to someone, their circle
sits on the right.

### How to check something off

1. **Tap the circle** on the left of the to-do.
2. It gets a green ✓ and moves into the **Done** group.

> **Repeating to-dos come back.** Checking off a repeating to-do (like *Water
> plants — weekly*) only counts for today; it returns on schedule next time.

### How to see finished to-dos

- Tap **Show done** near the bottom to reveal completed items. Tap **Hide
  done** to tuck them away again.

### How to add a to-do

1. Tap the **+** button in the bottom-right corner.
2. Type **What** it is (e.g. *Call the plumber*).
3. **Who** (optional) — tap **Anyone**, or tap a person's circle to assign it.
4. **Due date** (optional) — pick a date, or leave it blank for "Someday."
5. **Repeats** (optional) — choose a schedule to make it come back regularly.
6. **Category** (optional) — like *Home*, *School*, *Errands*, or *Work*
   (type your own, too).
7. **Notes** (optional) — any extra details.
8. Tap **Save**.

### How to edit or delete a to-do

1. **Tap the to-do's words** to open it.
2. Change anything and **Save**, or tap **Delete** and confirm.

### How to see just one person's to-dos

1. Tap a person's circle at the top of the page.
2. You'll see only their to-dos (plus shared ones). Tap the circle again for
   everyone.

### If something's off

- **The page says "Nothing on the list."** There are no to-dos yet — tap **+**
  to add the first one.
- **A to-do you finished keeps coming back.** It's a *repeating* to-do — that's
  by design. Edit it and set Repeats to "Does not repeat" if you want it to
  stop.

## 🍽️ Meals

**What it is:** The week's meal plan — a simple grid you fill in.

### What you'll see

- A **grid**: days of the week across the top, and three rows down the side —
  **🍳 Breakfast**, **🥪 Lunch**, **🍽️ Dinner**.
- Today's column is highlighted. Empty slots show a **+**; filled slots show
  the meal's name.

### How to plan a meal

1. **Tap any slot** (a day + breakfast/lunch/dinner box).
2. Type **What's cooking?** (e.g. *Taco night*). 🌮
3. Add **Notes** if you like (e.g. *use the slow cooker*).
4. Tap **Save**.

### How to move between weeks

1. Use the **‹** and **›** arrows at the top to change weeks.
2. Tap **This week** to jump back.

### How to send ingredients to a shopping list

1. Open a meal slot (tap it).
2. In the **"Need ingredients?"** box, type the ingredients — **one per
   line**.
3. If there's more than one list, pick which list to add them to.
4. Tap **Add to list**. The items land straight on that shopping list, and the
   button flashes **"Added ✓."**

### How to clear a meal

- Open the slot and tap **Clear** to empty it.

### If something's off

- **There's no "Add to list" area.** You need at least one shopping list first
  — make one on the [Lists](#-lists) screen.

## 🛒 Lists

**What it is:** Shopping lists (and any other lists you want) — Groceries,
Costco, hardware store, and so on.

### What you'll see

- **Tabs across the top**, one per list, each with a small **count** of how
  many items are still unchecked.
- The list itself below: unchecked items first, then a **done** section.

### How to switch lists

- Tap the **tab** for the list you want. The highlighted tab is the one you're
  viewing.

### How to make a new list

1. Tap **+ New list**.
2. Type a **Name** (e.g. *Groceries*, *Costco*, *Hardware*).
3. Pick an **Emoji** if you like.
4. Tap **Save**.

### How to add an item

1. Tap the box that says **"Add to \[list name]…"**.
2. Type the item.
3. Tap **Add** (or press Enter on a keyboard).

### How to use the quick-add chips

- Under the add box you may see **suggestion chips** — things we buy a lot.
  Tap one (e.g. **+ Milk**) to add it instantly. Handy for the usual staples.

### How to check items off while shopping

1. **Tap the circle** on the left of an item to check it off. It moves to the
   **done** section.
2. Tapped one too soon? Tap it again to bring it back.

### How to say who's picking something up

1. On an item, tap the **little ＋ circle** on the right.
2. It shows the first person's picture. Tap it again to move to the **next
   person**, and again to cycle through everyone — one more tap clears it back
   to nobody.
3. Stop when it shows the right person. 🛒

### How to tidy up

- When you're done shopping, tap **Clear completed** to sweep away everything
  you checked off.

### How to rename or delete a list

1. With the list open, tap **Rename / remove**.
2. Change the name or emoji and **Save**, or tap **Delete list** and confirm.

### If something's off

- **No lists yet.** Tap **+ New list** — *Groceries* is a classic first list.
- **Lists are great from a phone**, too — see
  [From your phone](#-from-your-phone-and-a-future-app).

## 🌙 Sleep

**What it is:** Controls for what the panel does at night, and when the
slideshow kicks in on its own.

### What you'll see

Two sections: **Night sleep** and **Daytime slideshow**.

### How to set what happens at night

1. Under **"At night, the panel should…"**, choose one:
   - **Stay on** — keep showing the calendar all night.
   - **Go dark** — dim the screen so it's not glowing in the room.
   - **Show photos** — run the slideshow through the night.
2. If you picked *Go dark* or *Show photos*, set the hours:
   - **Sleep at** — when night mode begins (e.g. 10:00 PM).
   - **Wake at** — when it returns to normal in the morning (e.g. 7:00 AM).

### How to set the daytime slideshow

1. **Start the slideshow after … quiet minutes** — drag the slider to choose
   how long the panel should sit untouched before the slideshow starts on its
   own. Slide it all the way down to **never** if you don't want that.
2. **Each photo shows for … seconds** — drag to set how long each photo stays
   up before the next one.
3. Tap **▶ Preview slideshow** to see how it looks. Tap the screen to exit.

### How to wake a sleeping panel

- **Just tap the screen.** It wakes up for a few minutes, then drifts back to
  sleep on its own.

### If something's off

- **The panel dimmed during the day.** That's the idle slideshow/sleep at
  work. Tap it to wake it, and adjust the timings here if it's too eager.

## ⏱ Timer

**What it is:** A big, friendly countdown timer for "ten minutes until we
leave!" moments.

### What you'll see

- Tap the **⏱** button in the top bar. A setup screen appears with **preset
  buttons** and a place to type your own minutes.
- Once running, a **large countdown ring** shrinks as time ticks down, with
  the minutes and seconds in the middle.

### How to start a timer

1. Tap **⏱** in the top bar.
2. Tap a preset — **1, 5, 10, 15, or 30 minutes** — *or* type a number in the
   **Custom minutes** box (up to 180) and tap **Start**.
3. The countdown ring begins. ⏳

### How to pause or stop

- Tap **Pause** to freeze it, then **Resume** to continue.
- Tap **Cancel** to stop and close the timer.

### What happens at zero

- The timer **chimes** (a few beeps), the ring turns red, and it shows
  **"Time's up!"**. Tap anywhere (or tap **Done**) to dismiss it.

### If something's off

- **No sound at zero.** The screen still flashes and shows "Time's up!" even if
  this particular panel can't play sound — you won't miss it.

## 📣 Notes (announcements)

**What it is:** Sticky notes that appear on the main screen — *"Dinner's
ready!"*, *"Dog walked ✓"*, *"Gone to practice."*

### What you'll see

- Posted notes appear as a **strip at the top of the Calendar screen**, each
  with an emoji, the message, and who it's from.

### How to post a note

1. Tap the **📣** button in the top bar.
2. Type your message (e.g. *Dinner's ready!*).
3. Pick an **emoji** for it (📌 🍝 🎉 ❤️ 🚗 🧹 ⚠️ 🎂).
4. Choose **From** — tap your circle so everyone knows who wrote it (optional).
5. Choose how long it **stays up**: **1 hour**, **4 hours**, **Today**, or
   **Until removed**.
6. Tap **Post it**. Your note appears on the main screen. 📌

### How to take a note down

- Tap the **✕** on the note. Anyone can remove any note.

### How to also email the family

- If email is set up **and** we have recipients saved, you'll see an **"Also
  email the family"** checkbox in the post box. Tick it to send the note to
  everyone's inbox as well.

### If something's off

- **No "Also email the family" option.** Email isn't set up yet, or no
  recipients have been added — a grown-up can sort that in
  **Settings → Daily email digest**.

## 🌤️ Weather

**What it is:** The forecast — a quick peek in the top bar, and a full page for
the details.

### What you'll see

- In the top bar, a small **weather chip** with an icon and the current
  temperature.
- Tapping it opens the full **Weather** page: current conditions (feels-like,
  humidity, wind) and a multi-day forecast with highs, lows, and chance of
  rain.

### How to see the full forecast

1. **Tap the temperature** in the top bar.
2. Read the current conditions and the day-by-day outlook.

### Weather alerts

- A **red dot** on the weather chip means there's an active weather alert.
- Open the Weather page to read the **alert details** (they show at the top,
  with what it is and when it ends). ⚠️

### If something's off

- **No weather at all.** Weather needs a location set and a weather service
  connected. A grown-up can set the location in **Settings → Household** (and
  connect the service via the setup guide if needed).

## ⚙️ Settings (grown-ups)

**What it is:** The grown-up controls — appearance, family members, reminders,
security, email, and backups.

> 🔒 If the **settings lock** is on, Canopy asks for the **family PIN** before
> opening this screen.

### Appearance

- **Theme** — tap a theme to switch instantly. **Skylight** is the classic
  bright look; there's also **Dark**, **Bold**, and **Pride** 🌈, plus
  light/dark variants and a *Match device* option that follows the screen's own
  light/dark setting.
- **Glass effect** — drag the slider to make the panels more or less
  see-through (0% is solid, 100% is the glassiest).

### Household

Three fields, each with its own **Save** button that appears when you make a
change:

- **Family name** — shown in the header.
- **Location** — the town/zip used for weather. Saving it refreshes the
  forecast right away.
- **Device name** — a friendly name for this panel.

### Family members

- See everyone in the family, each with their color and picture.
- **+ Add family member** — type a **Name**, pick a **Color** (each person
  gets a different one), and choose an **Avatar** emoji (or use their initial).
- **Edit** changes a person; **Remove** takes them off. Removing someone keeps
  their chores and tasks but leaves them unassigned.

### Reminders

- Drag the slider to choose how many **minutes before an event** the panel
  should pop up an on-screen reminder. Slide it all the way down to turn event
  reminders **off**.

### Security

- **Set / Change the family PIN** (4–8 digits). The PIN is what lets phones and
  laptops on your home Wi-Fi use Canopy. With no PIN set, **only this panel**
  can use Canopy.
- **Require the PIN to open Settings on this panel** — tick this to lock the
  Settings screen behind the PIN.

### Daily email digest

*(Only appears once email is connected on the server.)*

- Tick **"Send a morning 'today at a glance' email"** to get a daily email with
  today's events and to-dos.
- **Send at** — the time it goes out each morning.
- **Recipients** — the email addresses that receive it (separate several with
  commas).
- **Send test** — sends a sample email so you can confirm everything works. The
  test goes to your **configured digest recipient(s)**, so **add at least one
  recipient first**, then tap **Send test** and check that inbox.

### Backup

- **Download backup** — saves a file with all your **settings and family
  members**. It contains **no PINs and no Google keys**, so it's safe to keep
  anywhere.
- **Restore from file…** — pick a backup file to load those settings and people
  back in.
- **Cloud backup** (only appears if a grown-up connected it) — saves a **full
  copy of everything** (chores, stars, lists, meals, calendars, notes…) to the
  cloud **automatically every day**. You'll see when the last backup happened.
  - **Back up now** makes an extra copy this instant.
  - **Restore from cloud…** brings back the most recent copy if the panel ever
    loses its data — it asks you to confirm first, because it replaces
    everything currently on the panel.

### Calendars

- Calendars aren't managed here — they live on the **Calendar** screen. Tap
  **Manage** there to add a Google calendar (two-way sync) or subscribe to a
  school/team calendar by link (read-only).

### If something's off

- **Settings asks for a PIN and you don't know it.** Ask whoever set up the
  panel. The lock can be turned off again under **Security** once you're in.
- **The digest section shows a setup note instead of options.** Email isn't
  connected on the server yet — that's a one-time setup a grown-up does with
  the setup guide.

## 📱 From your phone (and a future app)

**What it is:** Canopy from your own phone or laptop — add groceries from the
couch, check the calendar from bed. 🛋️

### How to open Canopy on your phone

1. Make sure your phone is on the **same home Wi-Fi** as the panel.
2. Open any web browser and go to the **panel's address** — ask whoever set it
   up; it's usually something like **`http://canopy.local:3000`**.
3. Enter the **family PIN** and you're in.
4. Now you can add to shopping lists, check the calendar, post notes, and more
   — all from your hand.

### If it says remote access is off

- That means the **family PIN hasn't been set yet**. On the panel itself, a
  grown-up can set one under **Settings → Security**. Once there's a PIN,
  phones and laptops can connect.

### A possible future app

Down the road, a dedicated **phone/companion app** could connect to this very
